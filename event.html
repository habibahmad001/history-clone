1975

Labor leader Jimmy Hoffa is reported missing

By History — August 21, 1975

On July 30, 1975, labor leader Jimmy Hoffa was reported missing from outside a restaurant in suburban Detroit. Hoffa had been trying to regain control of the Teamsters Union after serving a prison term.

Investigation

The disappearance sparked an extensive investigation and numerous theories surrounding his fate. His disappearance remains one of the most famous unsolved mysteries in U.S. history.

Legacy

Hoffa’s legacy is a complicated mix of labor achievements and legal controversy. This event continues to capture public interest decades later.
